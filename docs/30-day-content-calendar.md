# BestKarma 30-Day Content Calendar

Date: March 29, 2026
Built from: the published BestKarma article library, current site positioning, and the marketing strategy in [docs/marketing-strategy.md](/Users/pareshchauhan/ai/projects/bestkarma/docs/marketing-strategy.md)

## Goal

Use the next 30 days to prove three things:

1. which topics get the most saves and shares
2. which content formats create the most newsletter signups
3. which audience wedge responds most strongly to BestKarma

This plan is designed to be practical for a small team. It assumes:

- 1 weekly newsletter
- 3 carousel posts per week
- 2 short-form videos per week
- 2 stat/quote/community posts per week

That gives you 8 posts per week plus the newsletter.

## Primary Focus For This 30-Day Sprint

Use this audience lens across the month:

`thoughtful adults, especially women 35-55, who want science-backed health and longevity guidance without hype`

Use these flagship franchises:

- `BestKarma Evidence Check`
- `30-Second Science`
- `Women’s Healthspan Notes`

## Offer To Push In Every CTA

Use one consistent call to action for the month:

`Subscribe to BestKarma Weekly for one science-backed insight and one practical action every Sunday.`

If you create a lead magnet during this month, upgrade the CTA to:

`Subscribe to get the BestKarma Healthspan Starter Kit plus one practical insight every Sunday.`

## Content Mix Rules

Across the 30 days:

- 40% sleep and stress
- 30% women’s healthspan
- 20% everyday longevity
- 10% nutrition/metabolism

Reason:

- sleep and stress are your easiest viral entry points
- women’s healthspan is your strongest strategic differentiation
- longevity builds authority but often needs simpler packaging

## Weekly Rhythm

Use a repeatable cadence:

- Monday: carousel
- Tuesday: short video
- Wednesday: carousel
- Thursday: stat card or conversation post
- Friday: short video
- Saturday: carousel
- Sunday: newsletter plus one supporting promo post

## Fast Production Workflow

To keep this manageable, use one article repurposing workflow:

1. Pull the article title and TL;DR.
2. Extract 3-5 strongest claims or facts.
3. Write one social-native hook.
4. Translate one practical takeaway.
5. Add one CTA:
   - save
   - send to a friend
   - subscribe

## Best Hooks To Reuse

- `Most people think X. The evidence says Y.`
- `This health problem starts earlier than most people realize.`
- `The treatment that works best is often not the one people hear about most.`
- `A lot of wellness advice sounds smart. This is the part that actually matters.`
- `This is one of the most useful health ideas almost nobody explains clearly.`
